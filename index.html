Entebbe, Uganda

Entebbe is a serene lakeside city nestled on a peninsula jutting into the vast waters of Lake Victoria, the world's largest tropical lake. Once the colonial capital of Uganda, it serves today as the country's international gateway, home to Uganda's main international airport. The city enjoys a lush, green landscape fed by a humid equatorial climate, and is known for its botanical gardens — among the oldest in East Africa — as well as wildlife sanctuaries that shelter endangered chimpanzees. Despite being just 37 kilometres from the bustling capital Kampala, Entebbe retains a quieter, more relaxed character, with tree-lined streets, waterfront views, and a pace of life that reflects Uganda's broader reputation as the "Pearl of Africa."
